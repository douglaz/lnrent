# 0016 — The ledger authorizes; the balance only reconciles

The daemon holds one pooled Fedimint ecash balance that commingles operator earnings with money
owed to buyers (pending refunds, receipts for orders that can still fail and refund). Every money
decision — "may the operator sweep X?", "can we cover our refund liabilities?" — needs a "what do
we have vs what do we owe" answer, and the obvious primitive is reading the wallet:
`available_balance_msat()`.

That primitive is a trap. The federation balance is a *derived cache* of the same operation
history the daemon's sqlite ledger records — computed on the federation's clock, updated
asynchronously as payments settle. Authorizing from it means reconciling two views of one truth
across two clocks, and that race surface is unbounded: a settlement can sit in the balance before
capture books its liability (sweepable-drain window); a payment can land between any catch-up scan
and any balance read; a late settlement on a locally-expired invoice is owed as a refund before
any row says so. The first draft of the sweep spec authorized from the balance and a 9-pass
adversarial review kept finding one more interleaving — catch-up-first, balance-before-reserve
read ordering, reserving all open invoices, quiescence refusals — each patch papering over the
same root mistake. The operator named it: **the balance is meaningless; only transactions are
meaningful.**

## Decision

The sqlite ledger (captured receipts, `refund_attempt`, `sweep_attempt`, subscription states) is
the **sole authority** for every money decision and every automatic warning. The federation
balance is **never read implicitly** — not by the sweep gate, not by refund readiness, not by
floor warnings, not at backend startup.

- **Sweep authorization** is a pure ledger computation:
  `surplus = Σ captured receipts − reserved (at-risk receipts + non-terminal refunds, at gross,
  de-duped per external_id) − paid_out (SENT refunds at gross + sweep outlay caps)`,
  where "captured receipts" spans both provenance classes INV-3 recognizes (settled invoice rows
  and unmatched/orphan settlement journal entries), **de-duped by external payment id** — a
  settlement recorded in both classes counts once — so receipts and their refunds always net over
  the same set.
  A receipt counts toward surplus only when no state-machine path can still route it to a refund;
  when in doubt it is reserved (fail closed).
- **Refund readiness (INV-2)** compares its liability requirement against the ledger-derived
  expected-holdings lower bound (receipts − refunds SENT-or-locally-started at gross − sweep
  caps), not a live balance query. The `BalanceQueryFailed` alarm class is retired — there is no
  automatic query left to fail.
- **The single sanctioned balance read** is the explicit, operator-invoked, report-only
  `lnrent reconcile`: it compares the wallet against the ledger lower bound and reports OK/DRIFT
  for a human. It never gates a payment and never auto-acts.
- **The pay itself is the fail-safe** for book-vs-wallet shortfall: if the wallet somehow holds
  less than the ledger authorized (a federation-level loss or a ledger bug), assembling the ecash
  notes fails cleanly before anything moves. A pre-read could only have detected — never safely
  fixed — that state.

Accounting is deliberately conservative in one direction: refunds and sweeps are subtracted at
gross/cap while real outlays are ≤ that (INV-1), so ledger figures under-state holdings and can
never over-authorize.

## Considered options

- **Balance-authorized with reconciliation patches.** Read the balance, then close each race
  (force catch-up, order the reads, reserve open invoices, refuse during reconciliation).
  Rejected: the race class is generated by the read itself; the review loop demonstrated it
  empirically (9 passes, each fix revealing the next interleaving — vs 3 passes to clean once the
  read was removed). It also drags a network failure mode (`BalanceQueryFailed`) into every
  automatic money path.
- **Ledger-authorized, balance demoted to explicit reconciliation (chosen).** Uncaptured
  settlements simply aren't receipts yet — invisible to authorization until capture books receipt
  and liability together — so the races vanish by construction, not by patching. Cost: ledger
  figures lag the wallet slightly (freshly settled money becomes sweepable only after capture) and
  run conservatively low (fees counted at gross); both err toward refusing an operator payout,
  never toward touching buyer funds.

## Consequences

- `available_balance_msat()` keeps exactly one non-test call site (the reconcile handler); the
  Fedimint startup `log_readiness` balance probe is removed (its gateway-liveness half stays —
  liveness and pricing probes are execution concerns, not balance reads, and remain allowed).
- The landed INV-2 readiness path and `lnrent money` are revised to ledger figures
  (docs/specs/gate1-alerting-operability.md §E); `lnrent money` becomes network-free apart from
  liveness probes.
- Wallet-vs-books drift detection is a deliberate human act (`lnrent reconcile`), not an ambient
  daemon behavior — a DRIFT verdict means a federation-level loss or a ledger bug and warrants
  investigation, not automation.
- Specs: docs/specs/gate1-operator-sweep.md (authorization), gate1-alerting-operability.md §D–§F
  (holdings floor, INV-2 revision, reconcile) are the normative elaboration of this decision.
